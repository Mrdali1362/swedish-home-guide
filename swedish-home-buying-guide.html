Stage 1 · Financial Readiness

Am I ready
to buy a home?

Buying a home is Sweden's biggest financial decision. Before you fall in love with a property, let's make sure your finances are solid. This stage explains what banks look at — and gives you an honest readiness score.

What does it take to buy?

🏦
Lånelöfte (mortgage pre-approval)
Your bank reviews your income, debts, and expenses and tells you the maximum they'll lend. Without this, you can't bid. Get it before you start viewing.
💰
Handpenning (down payment)
As of April 2026, you need at least 10% of the purchase price in cash. On a 4,000,000 kr home, that's 400,000 kr you must have saved.
📊
KALP (residual income test)
Banks stress-test your finances at 7% interest. They check you'd still have enough left to live on. Even with a high income, too many debts fail this test.
🛡️
Buffer (cash reserve)
Aim for 3–6 months of expenses in savings after your down payment. Unexpected costs like repairs, moving, and furnishing hit fast after purchase.
Plain-language tip: Your bank cares about three numbers — how much you earn, how much you owe, and how much you've saved. The calculator below shows where you stand on all three.

Stage 2 · Affordability

What will it actually
cost each month?

The asking price is just the beginning. Every month you'll pay a mortgage, a housing fee (avgift), heating, and more. This stage helps you calculate the true monthly cost — and compare bostadsrätt vs house ownership side by side.

🏢
Bostadsrätt (BR)
You own the right to live in the apartment. A housing association (BRF) owns the building. You pay a monthly avgift that covers shared costs and the BRF's loans. The BRF's debt is your hidden risk.
🏠
Äganderätt / Villa
You own the property outright. No BRF debt, no hidden avgift surprises. But you pay all maintenance yourself. Banks add a 5,000–8,000 kr/month maintenance allowance in their KALP calculation.
📉
Ränteavdrag (tax deduction)
Sweden gives you a 30% tax deduction on mortgage interest (up to 100,000 kr/year). On 100,000 kr of interest, you get 30,000 kr back at tax time. This meaningfully reduces your real cost.
📈
Amortering (repayment)
You must repay 1–3% of your loan per year depending on the loan-to-value ratio. This isn't "wasted" money — it builds equity — but it affects your monthly cash flow.

Stage 3 · Property Search

Finding and evaluating
the right property

Once you know your budget, the search begins. This stage covers where to look, what to look for during viewings, and how to score neighbourhoods — so you stop going on gut feeling alone.

Where to search

🔍
Hemnet.se
Sweden's dominant property portal. Set alerts for your criteria and check daily — good properties in popular areas get 20+ viewings within days.
📍
Booli.se
Better historical price data. Great for seeing what similar homes sold for in the last 12 months — essential for bid planning.
⚠️
Tomträtt — watch out
Some properties don't include the land — the municipality owns it. You pay an annual tomträttsavgift that can increase dramatically. Always check "markägare."
🔒
Off-market (budgivning)
Not all properties appear online. Tell brokers you know exactly what you want. Pre-emptive bids before public listing are common and can help you avoid bidding wars.
Viewing checklist
✅ Viewing Checklist Check off as you walk through

Tap each item as you check it during the viewing. Progress saves automatically.

  • Check the energy rating (energiklass A–G on the listing)
    C or better is good. F–G means high heating bills.
  • Open every window and check seals
    Condensation between panes = failed seal, costs 3,000–8,000 kr each.
  • Check under kitchen sink for water damage or mould smell
    Dishwasher leaks are the #1 hidden damage source.
  • Ask when the bathroom was last renovated
    Over 25 years old? Budget 150,000–250,000 kr for renovation.
  • Test all radiators and check heating type
    District heating (fjärrvärme) is cheapest and most reliable.
  • Look at ceiling corners for water stains
    Yellow/brown rings = past or ongoing leak from above.
  • Check mobile signal and ask about broadband
    Fibre available? Speed? This affects daily life significantly.
  • Walk the neighbourhood at different times of day
    Traffic noise, parking, proximity to bars — varies by hour.
  • Ask the broker about upcoming BRF stämma decisions
    Planned major works = upcoming special fee (extrainsats).
  • Check storage allocation (förråd) — is one included?
    Some are assigned, some are bid for internally. Ask.

Stage 4 · Document Analysis

Reading the financial
documents — with AI help

The årsredovisning (annual report) tells you whether the housing association is financially healthy or quietly headed for trouble. Most buyers never read it. You will — and our AI will explain what it means in plain language.

📊
Räntekänslighet (interest sensitivity)
How much does the BRF's avgift rise if interest rates increase by 1%? Under 10% = excellent. Over 20% = serious risk. Brokers sometimes misquote this number — always verify.
💳
Skuld per kvm (debt per sqm)
The BRF's total debt divided by total living area. Under 5,000 kr/kvm = low risk. Over 15,000 = high. This debt is yours too — it affects your real purchase price.
🔧
Underhållsplan (maintenance plan)
A legally required long-term plan for repairs (roof, pipes, facade). Missing or outdated = major red flag. The BRF may have surprise costs with no reserves to cover them.
💼
Soliditet (equity ratio)
The BRF's own equity vs total assets. Over 15% = healthy. Under 5% = stretched. Very low soliditet + high debt = association at risk of raising fees significantly.

Stage 5 · Bid Strategy

Bidding with strategy,
not just nerves

Swedish budgivning (bidding) is open and fast. Within 48 hours you may need to decide your maximum and how to reach it. This stage gives you a structured bid plan generated by AI — so you go in with a number, not a guess.

⚡
Budgivning (open bidding)
All bids are visible. The seller can accept any bid at any time or take the property off the market. There is no legal obligation until the contract is signed.
🎯
Pre-emptive bids
You can bid before the open bidding starts. The seller may accept immediately, avoiding a bidding war. Requires showing financial strength — attach your lånelöfte.
🚧
Walk-away price
Decide your maximum before bidding starts — not in the heat of the moment. Factor in renovation costs from your viewing. Exceed this number and the purchase becomes financially unsound.
📋
Kontrakt (contract)
Only binding once both parties sign. You pay 10% handpenning at signing. If you back out after signing, you typically lose the handpenning. Don't sign until you're certain.

Stage 6 · Closing

From accepted bid
to getting the keys

Your bid was accepted — congratulations. Now comes the final sprint: contracts, bank paperwork, and moving in. Here's what happens, when, and what you must not forget.

📝
Bostadsrätt: No lagfart needed
For bostadsrätt, there is no property registration with Lantmäteriet. The BRF registers you in their lägenhetsförteckning. Your bank secures the loan via pantsättning — much simpler and cheaper than buying a house.
🏡
Äganderätt: Lagfart required
For a villa/house, you register ownership with Lantmäteriet within 3 months of signing. Stämpelskatt = 1.5% of purchase price (or assessed value). Pantbrev = 2% if new. Budget 50,000–100,000 kr extra.
⏱️
Tillträde (possession date)
Typically 2–3 months after signing. Negotiate this — sellers often want maximum time, buyers minimum. Make sure your mortgage offer and current lease/sale align with this date.
🔑
Besiktning (inspection)
For äganderätt, always get a professional besiktning before signing. For bostadsrätt it's less common but worth requesting for older properties or parhus/radhus where you own more of the structure.
🗂️ Closing Checklist From accepted bid to moving in
  • Request contract draft (köpekontrakt) from broker
    Read carefully before signing. Check price, tillträde date, and any conditions.
  • Verify all seller disclosures (upplysningsplikten)
    Any known defects must be disclosed. Water damage, mould, radon — ask explicitly.
  • Confirm tillträde date suits your situation
    Check your current lease notice period and bank loan offer expiry.
  • Transfer handpenning (10%) on signing day
    Via bank transfer, not cash. Keep confirmation receipt.
  • For äganderätt: book professional besiktning
    Book before signing if possible — or include a besiktningsklausul in the contract.
  • Confirm final mortgage amount and terms with bank
    Your lånelöfte is not a loan — you still need final credit approval.
  • Decide on fixed vs variable rate
    Fixed gives certainty. Variable (rörlig) is historically cheaper but riskier. Split is common.
  • Set up automatic amortering payments
    Minimum 1% for LTV 50–70%, 2% for 70–85%, 3% for 85–90%.
  • For bostadsrätt: BRF registers pantsättning
    Bank communicates directly with BRF. Pantsättningsavgift ~500–1,000 kr.
  • For äganderätt: apply for lagfart within 3 months
    Bank usually handles this. Budget 1.5% stämpelskatt of purchase price.
  • Book moving company 4–6 weeks ahead
    Demand peaks end of month. Book early or move mid-month for savings.
  • Register address change (folkbokföring) at Skatteverket
    Do this online at verksamt.se within one month of moving. Affects tax, voting, mail.
  • Update home insurance (hemförsäkring)
    Cancel old policy from move-out date. New policy from move-in date. Compare: IF, Trygg-Hansa, Folksam.
  • Switch electricity / broadband supplier
    Give 1–2 months notice. Nordnet, Vattenfall, Tibber for electricity. Check fibre availability first.
  • Change locks on move-in day
    You don't know how many copies of the key exist. Budget 500–2,000 kr for a locksmith.
  • Reserve moving lift (hiss/lastbrygga) with BRF
    Many BRFs require advance booking. Contact styrelsen or bokning@brf.
